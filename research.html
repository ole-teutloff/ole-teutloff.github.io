Ole Teutloff

AI and labour markets, inequality and computational social science at University of Oxford.

Research

I study how artificial intelligence is reshaping work, skills, and inequality. My research combines sociology, data science, and economics to trace how technologies like large language models reorganize labor markets and everyday work practices. I use computational methods—including natural language processing and network analysis—to measure who benefits from AI and who is left behind. I’m currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Oxford, supported by the Carlsberg Foundation. For a list of all my publications, see my portfolio here.
